# 🚨 Microsoft Defender XDR – Manage Incidents (Summary)

## 📌 Overview
An **incident** is a collection of related alerts that show the full story of an attack.

✅ Purpose:
- Correlate alerts across systems  
- Provide full attack visibility  
- Enable faster investigation and response  

---

## 🧠 Key Concept

✅ Incident = Multiple Alerts Combined  

- Alert → Single suspicious event  
- Incident → Full attack story  

✅ Benefit:
- See how attack started, spread, and impacted environment  

---

## 🔍 What Incidents Show

- Entry point of attack  
- Attack techniques used  
- Affected users, devices, mailboxes  
- Scope and severity  
- Attack progression (timeline)  

---

## ⚙️ Automation (AIR)

✅ Microsoft Defender can:
- Automatically investigate alerts  
- Take remediation actions  
- Use AI to resolve threats  

---

## 🖥 Incident Queue

Step 1 > Open Incidents page  
Step 2 > View last 30 days (default)  
Step 3 > Sort by severity / time  
Step 4 > Identify high-risk incidents  

✅ Shows:
- Priority incidents  
- Status (Active / Resolved)  
- Impact level  

---

## 🎯 Prioritise Incidents

Step 1 > Check severity (High = urgent)  
Step 2 > Review affected entities  
Step 3 > Analyse scope  
Step 4 > Focus on highest impact  

✅ Tip:
High severity + multiple entities = top priority  

---

## 🔎 Filters Available

Use filters to narrow incidents:

- Status (Active / Resolved)  
- Severity (Low / Medium / High)  
- Assignment (Me / Automation)  
- Source (Endpoint, Identity, Email)  
- Tags  
- Categories  
- Devices / Users  
- Data sensitivity  

✅ Helps focus on most critical threats  

---

## 👁 Incident Preview

### Preview Options

- ⭕ Circle → View quick details  
- > Expand → View related records  
- 🔗 Link → Open full incident  

✅ Allows quick investigation without opening full page  

---

## 🛠 Manage Incidents

Step 1 > Select incident  
Step 2 > Investigate alerts  
Step 3 > Take actions  

You can:

- Rename incident  
- Assign to analyst  
- Change status  
- Add tags  
- Add comments  

---

### ✏️ Rename Incident

Step 1 > Edit name  
Step 2 > Apply naming convention  

✅ Improves clarity and reporting  

---

### 👤 Assign Incident

Step 1 > Click Assign to me  
Step 2 > Take ownership  

✅ Ensures accountability  

---

### ✅ Set Status

- Active → Ongoing investigation  
- Resolved → Threat handled  

✅ Resolving closes all related alerts  

---

### 📊 Classification

- True positive → Real threat  
- False positive → Not a threat  

✅ Helps improve future detections  

---

### 📝 Add Comments

Step 1 > Add investigation notes  
Step 2 > Track history  

✅ Enables collaboration across SOC team  

---

### 🏷 Add Tags

Step 1 > Add custom labels  
Step 2 > Use for filtering later  

✅ Example tags:
- Phishing  
- Malware  
- Lateral movement  

---

## 🔄 Modify Incident Alerts

Step 1 > Go to Alerts tab  
Step 2 > Move alerts between incidents  

✅ Helps group related activity  

---

## ✅ Investigation Flow

Open Incident > Review Alerts > Analyse Timeline > Assess Impact > Take Action > Close  

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaways

- Incidents provide full attack visibility  
- Prioritize based on severity + impact  
- Use filters for faster analysis  
- Automation reduces response time  
- Proper management improves SOC efficiency  

---
